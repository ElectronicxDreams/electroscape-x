ELECTROSCAPE — QUICK START GUIDE
================================

1. FAVOURITES: ADDING & REMOVING tracks
---------------------------------------
• Click the HEART ICON (♥) on any track card in the main grid to add it to your Favourites.
• The heart will turn SOLID CYAN to indicate it is saved.
• To remove a track, simply click the heart again.
• Favourites are saved locally in your browser and will persist even if you close the page.


2. THE FAVOURITES SIDEBAR
-------------------------
• Click the [ FAVOURITES ] button in the top action bar to open your list.
• Inside the sidebar, you can:
    - PLAY ENTIRE LIST: Click the "[ PLAY PLAYLIST ]" button to start your Favourites queue.
    - REORDER: Use the DRAG HANDLE (6 dots) on the left of any row to drag and reorder tracks.
               Alternatively, use the UP/DOWN ARROWS (▲ / ▼) on each row to shift positions.
    - INDIVIDUAL PLAY: Click the small PLAY ICON (▶) on a row to jump directly to that track.
    - REMOVE: Click the "X" button on any row to remove that track from your Favourites.
    - CLEAR ALL: Click the "[ Clear All ]" button at the bottom to reset your list.


3. EXITING THE FAVOURITES QUEUE
-------------------------------
• When playing your Favourites list, the player is in "Favourite Mode."
• To EXIT this mode and return to the full Main Sequence:
    - Simply click ANY TRACK CARD in the main grid. 
    - This will immediately reset the player's queue to include all tracks from the main grid.


4. NAVIGATION & PLAYBACK
------------------------
• PREV / NEXT: Use the arrow buttons [<] and [>] in the action bar or HUD to step through the active queue.
• SHUFFLE (OVERRIDE SEQUENCE): Click "[ Override Sequence ]" to randomize the order of the main grid and reset the queue.
• ORBITAL HUD: Use the persistent controls at the bottom of the screen to toggle play/pause, seek through the track, or jump between songs.


5. VIEW CONTROLS
----------------
• Use the GRID ICONS at the top to change the display density:
    - MICRO (4x4): Smallest cards, most tracks on screen.
    - CORE (3x3): Balanced view (Standard).
    - MAX (2x2): Large cards for a better look at thumbnails.
